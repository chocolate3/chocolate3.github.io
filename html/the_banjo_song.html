This song is one of my favorites, especially considering it’s the only song I have written. I started with the lyrics, which were composed over the course of around 2 days. I started with the first few lines, “If the monster under your bed surrendered/would you stay with me?” I had a really basic melody thought out on the guitar. I’ve always enjoyed the music of Julian Koster. That’s why I picked up the banjo. I decided to take off the fifth string, but ended up tuning to plectrum tuning. I learned 3 chords whose names I forgot as soon as I played them. I loved their sound, the way the instrument resonated and the way I sorta could feel the vibrations on my chest. I love it. But I found these weird lyrics I’d written weeks earlier, and they fit perfectly with what I thought was just another unrealized jumble of phrases.

These photos by Cayman G. were all taken during rehearsals for my February 16th show, and I was very unprepared and hadn’t even memorized my lyrics.

The cover to the song, It’s from 1904 and I found it in a thrift store for a few dollars. I absolutely love old photos like this, and it was just what I had around and thought was nice

I loved writing it, and it was the first time I really was satisfied with a song. It made me feel good, truly good about something I did. I still feel that way, which is really cool. I wrote the rest of the lyrics in a 30 minute period of creative drive. I recorded it 2 days later and put it on Bandcamp that night. The funny thing is that I had just assembled this entire studio weeks earlier, yet only used a single microphone to record it, and did so in 2 takes. I played it live in my basement on February 16th, 2019. The video is on Youtube, and I am gonna try to put it here someday. Check out these cool images!
