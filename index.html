Welcome!!!

Hey y’all. So I’m Maxwell. I play 5 instruments pretty badly. Those are the banjo, guitar, organ, bass, and ukulele. I occasionally write and record music, but I never release it cause I’m really nervous. But hey, who cares? I have it for me. I play some guitars, that’s my main instrument. I’ll probably make a page just for them, but I mainly play an Epiphone ES-335, Danelectro 59 NOS, and a Stratocaster.

I also really like to collect random stuff, which is kinda why I made this site. I used to have a couple hundred cameras, but a lot of them are in storage and I never actually counted them. As I sit here, I have a 1955 Rolleicord right by me. I got it from my racist grandfather, Marvin. He’s actually known for his confederate belt buckles, but he got it for me at auction for 70 bucks. I still have the number of a competing bidder.

Me at school during the filming of a project

I also love old video equipment. I shoot a lot on VHS, Hi8, and MiniDV. I’d shoot EIAJ-1 if I had the budget. I also love tube cameras, like I love my Newvicon camera, but I used to have a sweet vidicon one from ‘82. It’s okay though. I should have a Magnavox B/W camera this Thursday from about ‘75. However, I also adore modern video equipment. I do a ton of video for my school. I’d share some but I’m not legally allowed to post it. I’ve rented out our really good DSLR twice now, but I forgot the SD card at school so I can’t use it over the break until I buy one for myself.

A (real!) Polaroid of me playing slide, then one of me messing with some old equipment I got from a family friend. Then, a photo I developed at home.

So, I think the photos above deserve some more explanation. I collect film cameras, as I’ve mentioned. I spent about a month in 2017 developing film at home using the equipment you see me playing around with. Then, I got some terrible quality photos from it. Hurray! But in all seriousness, I think my entire goal is to find more difficult ways to do things. That’s just how I suppose the trade is.

I guess I should mention some of my other hobbies. I’ve been doing calligraphy for a while now. I love scripts from around 600 BCE to 1800 CE. I also am extremely intrigued by mechanical reproduction of text. That’s basically a fancy way of saying I like printing presses and, in my opinion, their poor yet more affordable cousin, the typewriter. Though, that takes a lot of credit away from the typewriter, as I love them dearly. I’m currently actually using a new typewriter, a Sears Citation. Only 15 bucks on Goodwill’s Online Store. It’s actually my first properly working typewriter, the others are pretty broken. The oldest of them is from 1910, the newest from the 90s. I suppose I’ll leave this part of the site for now, this section is getting very long.
